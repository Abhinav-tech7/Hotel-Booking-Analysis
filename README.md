# 🏨 Hotel Booking Cancellation Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes hotel booking data from **City Hotel** and **Resort Hotel** to identify the key factors that influence booking cancellations. High cancellation rates can negatively affect hotel revenue and room utilization. The goal of this analysis is to understand cancellation patterns and provide data-driven insights that can help hotels improve pricing strategies and reduce cancellations.

The analysis includes **data cleaning, exploratory data analysis (EDA), and visualization** to uncover meaningful business insights.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem
Both City and Resort hotels experience a high number of booking cancellations, which leads to:
- Revenue loss
- Inefficient room utilization
- Difficulty in planning pricing strategies

This project aims to identify the main factors contributing to cancellations and provide recommendations to minimize them.

---

## ❓ Research Questions
- What factors influence hotel booking cancellations?
- How does pricing impact cancellation rates?
- Which booking channels contribute the most to reservations?
- What seasonal trends affect hotel bookings and cancellations?

---

## 📊 Key Insights
- Around **37% of hotel bookings were canceled**, significantly impacting revenue.
- **Higher Average Daily Rate (ADR)** is associated with a higher cancellation rate.
- **August** has the highest number of bookings, while **January** shows the highest cancellation rate.
- Nearly **46% of bookings come from Online Travel Agencies (OTAs)**.
- Portugal recorded the highest number of cancellations among countries.

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations
- Implement **dynamic pricing strategies** to reduce high-price cancellations.
- Offer **discounts or promotional campaigns** during high cancellation months.
- Provide **special weekend and holiday pricing** for resort hotels.
- Improve service quality in regions with high cancellation rates.
- Encourage **direct bookings** to reduce dependency on online travel agencies.
